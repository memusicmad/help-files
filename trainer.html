1. What this app does

The PT Portal is where you run your gym business. You log every client session, plan workouts, track progress, and manage your client list from one screen. Your clients see their own side in a separate Client app.

You reach the portal at pt.141kg.com in any browser, or through the installed app on your phone. The layout adapts: a bottom bar on a phone, a side menu on a wide screen.

Your plan sets what you see. Some pages and buttons unlock on higher plans. Locked items show a padlock. Section 13 lists what each plan gives you.

2. Signing in

Open pt.141kg.com. The sign-in screen shows PT Portal and the tagline Train · Track · Progress.

Email and password

  • Type your email and password.
  • Tap Sign in.

Two-factor code

The portal asks for a six-digit code from your authenticator app every time you sign in. This protects your clients' data. The first time you sign in, the app walks you through linking an authenticator (Google Authenticator, Authy, or similar): scan the QR code, then enter the code it shows.

Forgot your password

  • Tap Forgot password?
  • Enter your email. The app emails you a numeric code.
  • Type the code, tap Continue, then set a new password (at least 10 characters, with an upper case letter, a lower case letter, and a number).
Idle sign-out. The portal signs you out after an hour of no activity. Sign back in to carry on.

3. Add the app to your phone

The portal runs in a browser, but you can add it to your home screen so it opens like an installed app. You do this once. There is nothing to download from an app store.

On iPhone or iPad

  • Open pt.141kg.com in Safari.
  • Tap the Share button, the square with an arrow pointing up.
  • Tap Add to Home Screen, then tap Add.
  • Open the app from the new icon on your home screen.

On Android

  • Open pt.141kg.com in Chrome.
  • Tap the three-dot menu, top right.
  • Tap Add to Home screen, or Install app, then confirm.
  • Open the app from the new icon on your home screen.

Why add it

  • Notifications work. On iPhone, alerts only arrive once the app sits on your home screen. A browser tab cannot show them, so a client logging their own session would pass you by.
  • It opens full screen. No browser bars, so you get the whole screen for logging.
  • One tap to launch. The icon opens the portal straight away, signed in.

4. Getting around

5. Help when you need it

Every page carries a question mark. Tap it and the portal explains the page you are standing on: what it does, the steps, the plan it needs, and the thing that catches people out.

On a phone the question mark sits in the header, next to the bell. On a wide screen it sits at the foot of the side menu, as Help with this page. The panel follows you. Open it on Reports and you get Reports. Open it on Setup, Exercises and you get the exercise library. Tap outside the panel, or press Escape, to close it.

6. Log a session

The Log tab is the core of the app. You pick a client, add exercises with their sets, then end the session.

6.1 Pick who you are logging for

Choose a client from the dropdown at the top. The list splits into Planned clients (anyone with a workout planned for today) and All Others.

Two clients at once. Tap 2 Clients to log a shared session for a pair. Each set records against the client you tag it to. Tap it again to drop back to one client. Two-client logging needs the Elite plan.

6.2 Add an exercise

  • Search for the exercise by name, or pick a body area first to narrow the list.
  • Enter the numbers for the set. The fields you see depend on the exercise: Weight (kg), Reps or Secs, and Time.
  • Tap Add set. The set appears in the session list above.

The app remembers the client's last numbers for that lift, so you start from where they left off. A progression chip shows whether they are up, level, or down on last time.

6.3 Set types

A row of toggles above the entry fields switches the set type before you save it. Bodyweight lifts default their weight to the client's body weight. You can override it per set.

Set typeWhat it records
Warm UpMarks the set as a warm-up so it sits apart from working sets. Needs Pro.
Drop setAdds up to three drops under one set. Tick Add another drop set, then Add third drop set. Needs Pro.
Superset / trisetChain two or three exercises as one block. Tick Add third exercise (triset) for three. Needs Pro.
Assisted / partial repsRecords assisted or partial reps on bodyweight lifts, plus band colour where the lift uses a band.

6.4 Effort, heart rate, and calories come from your client

You do not enter effort, heart rate, or calories in the logger. Your client records these in their own app: a session effort score (RPE 0 to 10), and their heart rate and calories where they wear a watch. Once logged, they show against the session in History and feed the Recovery and Ranking reports. The Clients page shows which of these each client has agreed to store.

6.5 Log a past session

Set the date to the day the session happened. Any date other than today marks a past session. Log the session as normal. It saves against that date, not today. Retro logging needs the Essential plan or higher. Below that the date is locked to today.

6.6 End the session

Tap End Session when you are done. The session saves and shows in History. Nothing is lost if you leave the tab midway: your entries hold as a draft until you end or clear them.

Clients can log offline. In their installed app a client can record a session with no signal. It reaches you, and feeds your reports, once they reconnect and it syncs.

7. History

History (Day view) is a calendar of everything logged. Days with sessions carry a dot.

Open a day

Tap a date to see its sessions as cards. Each card shows the client, the exercises, and the sets. Tap a card to expand it.

Edit a session

Open the card and use the ... menu for Edit date or Delete session. In edit mode you change any set inline, then Save or Cancel from the bar pinned to the bottom. Editing needs the Essential plan or higher.

Add to a session

Tap Add exercise to this session to log a lift you missed. The same set types apply: warm up, drop set, superset, triset.

Deleting is permanent. Delete session removes the session and its sets. There is no undo.

8. Reports

Reports has three sub-tabs: Trainer, Recovery, and Ranking, and Personal bests sits inside the Trainer report. Pick a client at the top; the report reads that client's data. Ranking opens on the Essential plan. Trainer, PBs, and Recovery need Pro.

8.1 Trainer

The Trainer report shows volume, session counts, effort mix, and progression over a range you choose. Use it to see how a client is trending and where to push next. Needs Pro.

8.2 PBs

PBs opens from the Trainer report and lists the client's best lifts, grouped by body area, and flags exercises where they are getting stronger or faster. Cardio carries its own bests: fastest pace banded by distance, and furthest distance. Tap a lift to see its history. Needs Pro.

8.3 Recovery

Recovery reads each body area's readiness from recent load and effort. Green means ready to train, amber means recovering. Set the recovery cost on the exercise itself, in Setup, to tune how fast its work clears. Needs Pro. An area with no recent data shows as ready. Absence of load is not the same as recovery.

8.4 Ranking

Ranking is your client leaderboard. Clients score across four pillars: Sessions, Volume, Progression, and Cardio. The same colours and rules run in the Client app, so what a client sees matches your view. A client only appears if they opted in to the leaderboard. You cannot opt them in for them. A client joins the board after 40 days of training history; until then they are absent and their own app shows a countdown.

9. Planning

Planning lets you build a workout before the session. Three sub-tabs run across the top: Plan, Trainer, Client.

Plan

Build one workout at a time. Pick a client and date, then add exercises, sets, and reps. One plan per client per day.

Move it where you need it

Move to Trainer to run the plan yourself in a live session. It shows in the Trainer list ready to log against. Send to Client to push the plan into the client's app, where it appears under My Plan for them to follow.

Planning needs the Essential plan or higher. Below that the tab stays locked.

10. Setup

Setup holds your client list and your exercise library. A third sub-tab, Import / Export, shows on a wide screen.

10.1 Clients

The Clients page lists everyone you train. A client needs three things before you can invite them to the Client app: a name, an email address, and a date of birth. The Add Client form asks for all three.

Add a client and invite them

  • Enter the client's name, email address, and date of birth.
  • Tap Add Client. They appear on the list below.
  • Tap Invite on their card. 141kg emails them a link to activate the Client app.
  • The card shows Invited, then Active once they set their password.
Clients must be 16 or over. The app blocks the invite below that age. There is no override.

If the Invite button will not go through, it tells you what is missing. Clients brought in through Import arrive with a name only, so add an email and a date of birth to their card before you invite them.

What else the card shows. Chips for the health data the client agreed to store: weight, heart rate, RPE, and calories. The client sets these, not you. Retire stops a client counting toward your plan; their history stays, and Rejoin brings them back. Delete only works before a client has logged any sets. After that, Retire is the option.

Client plans and your allowance. Each plan includes a number of active clients and caps the total you can hold. Adding a client past your included number shows the extra cost before you confirm. Prices are on the website and in email. The app names the consequence but does not quote the figure. Confirm charges from your billing email.

10.2 Exercises

Your exercise library carries three groups: Strength, Circuit, and Cardio. Strength exercises sit under body areas (Chest, Shoulders, Back, Legs, and the rest).

Add an exercise

  • Open a body area, or the Circuit or Cardio section.
  • Type the exercise name. The app detects common types and sets the right fields (weight, reps, time, bodyweight, assisted, band).
  • Pick a matching master exercise from the library where one exists, or add your own. Custom exercises need Pro.

Your library is your own copy. Editing an exercise here changes it for your clients only. Each exercise carries a recovery cost, its Intensity factor, that tunes how fast its work clears in Recovery. Custom body areas need Pro. The shared master library is available on Essential and above.

10.3 Import / Export

On a wide screen, use Import / Export to bring exercise or session data in, or take a copy out. Import creates any client it does not recognise, by name.

Overwrite mode is permanent. It deletes the selected clients' current history before importing the file. There is no undo.

11. Me

The Me tab holds three sections: Profile, Branding, and Security.

Profile

Your account details, your plan badge, and a Build chip showing the app version. Quote the build number if you report a problem.

Branding

Set your logo and brand colour. Your colour flows through both apps, so your clients see your brand, not a generic one. Branding needs Elite.

Security

Change your password. Set a new one at any time. Manage two-factor: add or remove an authenticator. At least one stays required.

12. Alerts

Alerts lists recent notifications, for example when a client logs their own session. Turn on push notifications to get them on your phone, and set the email cadence from the notification settings. On iPhone, add the app to your home screen first (Section 3) or push cannot reach you.
