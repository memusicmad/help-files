1. What this app is for

This app is your side of your training with your PT. You see every session your trainer logs for you, log your own workouts, watch your progress, and see where you rank against the rest of the gym.

You reach it at client.141kg.com in any browser, or through the installed app on your phone. It carries your trainer's logo and colours.

Some features unlock on a higher plan. Locked items show a padlock. Section 14 lists what each plan gives you. Your trainer sets your plan.

2. Getting started

Your trainer adds you, and 141kg emails you an invite. Setting up takes a minute.

  • Open the invite email and tap the link. It opens the app ready to activate.
  • Enter the code from the email, then tap Continue.
  • Set your password (at least 10 characters, with an upper case letter, a lower case letter, and a number), then tap Set password & continue.
  • Read and accept the Terms of Service and Privacy Notice. You cannot use the app without accepting them, because the service needs to process your data to work.
  • Choose what health data to store (see below).
  • Choose whether to join the leaderboard (see below).

The first time the app opens, a short welcome walks you through what is where. Skip it if you would rather get going. The question mark at the top of any page brings the same help back.

2.1 Your health data

To show your full training picture, the app can store your body weight, heart rate, perceived effort (RPE), and session calories. Under UK GDPR these count as health data, so the app asks for your consent before it stores them. You can change your mind later in Profile, Privacy. Say no and you still log workouts; you skip the effort and calorie parts.

2.2 The leaderboard

Your trainer ranks clients on a friendly leaderboard. Joining is your choice. Opt in and you pick a display name that other clients see. Opt out and you stay off it. Change this any time in Profile, Privacy.

3. Signing in

  • Open client.141kg.com.
  • Enter your email and password, then tap Sign in.

Forgot your password

  • Tap Forgot password?
  • Enter your email. The app emails you a code.
  • Type the code, tap Continue, then set a new password.

4. Add the app to your phone

The app runs in a browser, but you can add it to your home screen so it opens like an installed app. You do this once. There is nothing to download from an app store.

On iPhone or iPad

  • Open client.141kg.com in Safari.
  • Tap the Share button, the square with an arrow pointing up.
  • Tap Add to Home Screen, then tap Add.
  • Open the app from the new icon on your home screen.

On Android

  • Open client.141kg.com in Chrome.
  • Tap the three-dot menu, top right.
  • Tap Add to Home screen, or Install app, then confirm.
  • Open the app from the new icon on your home screen.

Why add it

  • Reminders reach you. On iPhone, notifications only arrive once the app sits on your home screen. A browser tab cannot show them, so a nudge to train or a plan from your trainer would pass you by.
  • It opens full screen. No browser bars, so you get the whole screen.
  • One tap to launch. The icon opens the app straight away, signed in.
  • You can train offline. The installed app logs sessions with no signal (see section 8).

5. Getting around

Four buttons sit along the bottom on a phone: Home, Sessions, Progress, My Plan. Your Profile sits in the header. A Log session button starts a new workout from anywhere.

ButtonWhat it opens
HomeYour snapshot: recent sessions, your rank, recovery, and recent bests.
SessionsEvery session, yours and your trainer's, as cards you can open.
ProgressCharts, personal bests, recovery, gains, and ranking. Needs Pro.
My PlanWorkouts your trainer sent you, ready to follow.
ProfileYour goals, body weight, data and consent settings, plan, and sign out.

6. Help when you need it

Every page carries a question mark in the header. Tap it and the app explains the page you are standing on: what it does, the steps, and the plan it needs.

The panel follows you. Open it on Progress, PBs and you get PBs. Open it on Profile, Privacy and you get your consent settings. Tap outside the panel to close it.

Anything about your training goes to your trainer. Anything about your data goes to admin@141kg.com.

7. Home

Home is your snapshot. It pulls together the last seven days, your rank, your recovery status, and your recent personal bests. Tap any card to jump to the full view behind it.

  • Last 7 days shows your recent training at a glance.
  • Rank pill shows where you sit on the leaderboard, if you joined it.
  • Recovery shows whether your body areas are ready to train.
  • Recent bests lists lifts where you hit a new best. Tap one to see its history.

8. Log your own session

Tap Log session to record a workout you did on your own. Your trainer sees it, tagged as self-logged, so it sits apart from the sessions they run with you. Logging works on every plan.

  • Tap Log session.
  • Add an exercise. Search by name or pick from the library.
  • Enter each set: weight and reps, or time. Cardio takes a distance and a time.
  • Add more exercises the same way.
  • Tick each set as you finish it, then End session.

Effort, heart rate, and calories

On the Pro plan you also rate your effort for the session (RPE 0 to 10). Each number carries a label, from 0 no effort to 10 maximal effort. If you told the app you wear a heart-rate monitor, you add your heart rate and calories too. On Essential you log exercises and sets only.

Health data needs your consent. Body weight, heart rate, RPE, and calories only save if you opted in during setup. Change this any time in Profile, Privacy. Turn it all off and new health-data logging stops, but your past data stays.

Logging offline

In the installed app you can log a session with no signal. Home, Sessions and Log work offline. Progress and My Plan need a connection and show a notice until you are back online, and your Profile is read-only offline.

Anything you log offline saves on your phone and syncs to your trainer the next time you have signal. Stay signed in if you are training somewhere without signal, because signing out clears the offline copy. The copy refreshes each time you open the app online.

9. Sessions

Sessions lists every workout as a card, newest first. A tag shows who logged it: your trainer, or you. Open a card to see the exercises, sets, and your effort score.

Editing your sessions

On the Elite plan you can edit your own self-logged sessions and backdate them to any date. Sessions your trainer logged stay read-only on your side.

10. Progress

Progress is where you watch yourself improve. It opens on a hub: a card at the top names the lift that has moved the most, and rows below open the detail behind each one. Progress needs the Pro plan.

ViewWhat it shows
All the numbersWeight lifted, session counts, cardio, and effort over a period you choose.
PBsYour personal bests, grouped by body area. Tap a lift to see its history.
RecoveryReadiness by body area. Green means ready to train, amber means still recovering.
GainsLifts where you are getting stronger or faster. Needs Elite.
RankingThe full leaderboard and where you sit on it.

10.1 Cardio bests

Cardio carries its own personal bests alongside the lifts: your fastest pace for a distance, and the furthest distance you have covered. Both sit in the PBs view.

10.2 Ranking

The leaderboard scores everyone across four pillars: Sessions, Volume, Progression, and Cardio. Your trainer sees the same board. You only appear if you joined the leaderboard in Profile, Privacy. New clients join the board after 40 days of training; until then you see a countdown.

11. Recovery

Recovery sits inside Progress, as one of its rows, and a card on Home links straight to it. It reads how hard you trained each body area and how long since you last worked it. Green means ready to train, amber means still recovering. Use it to pick what to train next. Needs Pro.

A body area you have never trained shows as ready. No load is not the same as recovered.

12. My Plan

My Plan holds workouts your trainer built and sent you. Open one to see the exercises, sets, and target reps, then follow it in the gym. Log it as you go so your trainer sees you did it.

Plan your own

On the Pro plan you can build your own workouts to follow, alongside the ones your trainer sends.

13. Profile

Profile holds your account and your choices. Six sections sit across the top.

SectionWhat it holds
ProfileYour name, your plan, and your account details.
BodyweightRecord your body weight and watch it over time. Bodyweight lifts use this figure as the starting weight for a set.
GoalsSet targets to aim for. The app tracks you against them. Needs Pro.
SettingsHow the app behaves: units, notifications, and what you see on Home.
PrivacyWhat health data you store, and whether you sit on the leaderboard.
AccountChange your password, or sign out.
Privacy is where you change your mind. Health data covers body weight, heart rate, effort, and calories. Turn it off and new logging of it stops; your past data stays until you ask for it to go. Leave the leaderboard and you drop off the board, for your trainer too.

14. Plans and what each unlocks

Three plans run from Essential up to Elite. Your trainer sets yours, and can fund an upgrade for you.

PlanWhat you get
EssentialSee the sessions your trainer logs, log your own, the full exercise library, and your session history with a weekly streak. Free.
ProEverything in Essential, plus progress charts and PBs, body areas and recovery, ranking and leaderboard, session naming, effort with heart rate and calories, your own plans, and goals.
EliteEverything in Pro, plus Gains, editing your self-logged sessions, backdating to any date, and priority support.

To upgrade, talk to your trainer. Prices and payment sit on the website and in email, not in the app.

Need help? Tap the question mark on any page. Contact your trainer for anything about your training, or email admin@141kg.com for anything about your data.
